Free California Lemon Law Case Review

Ten questions, two minutes. An honest answer about whether your vehicle qualifies.

What happens after I submit this form?

You will get a direct response from Alex Craig, the attorney, not a call center. If your vehicle looks like it qualifies, the next step is a free consultation to review your repair orders. If it does not qualify, you will be told that plainly, along with what would change the answer (another repair attempt, more days out of service, or different paperwork). Prefer to talk first? Call 831-262-2847.

Under Civil Code section 1794(d), a prevailing consumer's attorney fees are paid by the manufacturer. That is why this review is free and why a qualifying case costs you nothing up front.

Vehicle Qualification Form

Your information is confidential. Submitting this form does not create an attorney-client relationship, and no deadline is protected until a written engagement is signed. Do not delay acting on your claim while waiting for a response.

What documents should I gather while I wait?

Collect your repair orders, your purchase or lease contract, and your warranty booklet; those three items decide most lemon law cases. Repair orders matter most: each one shows the date, the mileage, your complaint, and the days the shop kept the vehicle. If you are missing any, the dealership's service department can reprint your history on request.
