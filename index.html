About Alex Craig

Experienced Counsel.
Considered Advice.

After graduating from McGeorge School of Law in 2009, I spent nine years as in-house counsel for two California companies. That work gave me a clear view of how employers approach workplace disputes — how complaints are investigated, how decisions are documented, and where employees most often need help navigating the process. Today, I bring that perspective to the other side of the table, advising employees throughout California's Central Coast and Central Valley.

My practice is focused on California employment law: wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, retaliation, wage and hour disputes, and severance review. I also handle estate planning and personal injury matters. Whatever brings you to my office, you can expect a careful assessment, an honest read on your options, and steady guidance through what comes next.

How I Work

What to Expect When You Call

An Insider's Perspective

Nine years inside corporate legal departments means I know how employers investigate, document, and respond to employee complaints — and where the gaps tend to be.

Direct Attorney Access

You work with me, not an assistant or a rotating cast of associates. Calls and emails come back to you from the attorney handling your matter.

Discretion & Confidentiality

Employment disputes are personal. Your consultation is confidential, and you are under no obligation after we talk. You are welcome to reach out simply to understand where you stand.

Responsive Communication

Legal matters are stressful enough without wondering whether your attorney got your message. I respond promptly and keep you informed at every stage.

Regional Coverage

Available throughout California's Central Coast and Central Valley — from Monterey and Salinas through Hollister, Watsonville, Gilroy, Modesto, and Merced.

Plain-English Counsel

Employment law is full of statutes, procedures, and deadlines. I explain what they mean for your situation in clear language so you can make informed decisions.
