DUI Defense

A DUI charge can result in license suspension, fines, and even jail time. We challenge every aspect of DUI cases, including:

  • Traffic Stop Validity - Did police have proper cause to stop you?
  • Field Sobriety Tests - Were tests administered correctly?
  • Breathalyzer Accuracy - Was the device properly calibrated and maintained?
  • Blood Test Procedures - Was proper chain of custody followed?
  • DMV Hearings - Fighting to protect your driving privileges
  • First-Time DUI - Seeking reduced charges or diversion programs

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I talk to the police?

You have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. Generally, you should not answer questions from law enforcement without first consulting with an attorney. Politely decline to answer questions and ask to speak with your lawyer.

What's the difference between a misdemeanor and felony?

Misdemeanors are less serious crimes punishable by up to one year in county jail. Felonies are more serious and can result in state prison sentences of more than one year. Some offenses are "wobblers" that can be charged as either, depending on circumstances.

Can I get my record expunged?

Many California convictions can be expunged (dismissed) after you complete probation. An expungement can help with employment and housing applications. Contact us to see if you qualify.
